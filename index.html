Interview Preparation

Master your narrative for PM and consulting roles

"Your 16 years aren't just experience — they're case studies. You don't need to invent stories; you need to tell the ones you already lived."

Coaching note for Dr. Evans

Leadership & Influence
  1. Tell me about a time you led a team through significant organizational change.
  2. Describe influencing stakeholders who didn't report to you to achieve a program goal.
  3. How have you built trust with federal program officers and community partners simultaneously?
  4. Walk me through managing competing priorities across multiple federal grants at once.
Impact & Results
  1. What is your most impactful program? How do you define and measure success?
  2. Describe turning around an underperforming program or contractor.
  3. Give an example where your data analysis changed a programmatic decision.
  4. How have you translated implementation science into on-the-ground improvements?
Problem Solving
  1. Describe the most complex compliance or corrective action challenge you've navigated.
  2. Tell me about redesigning a program mid-implementation due to shifting requirements.
  3. How do you build data systems from scratch for organizations with limited infrastructure?
Values & Motivation
  1. What drives your commitment to health equity and disparities elimination?
  2. How does your DHA research inform your program management approach?
  3. Why are you moving toward consulting? What excites you about this transition?
Program Management
  1. Walk through managing a federal grant from award through close-out. What checkpoints do you build?
  2. How do you structure a logic model? Walk through one from a previous role.
  3. What's your approach to performance monitoring plans and evaluations?
  4. How do you prioritize deliverables when resource constraints emerge mid-year?
Data & Analytics
  1. Describe using REDCap or CDC SAMS for federal reporting. Biggest challenges?
  2. How do you ensure data quality across multiple contractors submitting data?
  3. Walk through building an executive dashboard — what do you prioritize and why?
  4. How do you approach quantitative vs. qualitative data in program evaluation?

Lead with structure

Name your federal fluency
